# The Problem

Every year, the Asian Development Bank publishes a Trade Finance Gap report. The 2023 
figure was $2.5 trillion. That is the value of trade that went unfinanced — goods that 
could have been sold, shipments that could have moved, businesses that could have grown.

Most of that gap falls on small and medium businesses in Asia, Africa, and Latin America. 
Not because the invoices are fraudulent or the businesses are risky — but because the 
traditional financing system is expensive to operate at small scale.

### How traditional invoice financing works

A business completes a shipment, issues an invoice for $10,000 payable in 60 days, and 
then approaches a bank or factoring company for early payment.

The bank runs a credit check on the business, verifies the invoice, checks the buyer's 
creditworthiness, prepares documentation, and eventually offers 85–90 cents on the dollar 
with a processing fee on top. The business gets maybe $8,000–8,500 after two weeks of 
waiting — and still has to manage the repayment relationship.

Factoring companies are faster but charge 3–5% flat fees plus administrative costs. 
For a $10,000 invoice, that is $300–500 in fees for a 60-day advance. Annualized, 
that is 18–30% APR.

### What TrusTrove changes

The smart contract replaces the bank's verification and custody function. The Stellar 
ledger replaces the paper trail. USDC replaces the wire transfer.

The discount is set by the SME at the time of listing — not imposed by an institution 
after a credit review. The pool funds it instantly if liquidity exists. The buyer repays 
directly to the protocol at the due date. No one in the middle takes a cut.

At 2% discount on a 60-day invoice, the annualized cost is roughly 12%. That is below 
most credit card rates and well below traditional factoring — with no paperwork.
